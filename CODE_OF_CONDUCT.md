# Code of Conduct

AipeHub is a small project that wants to stay a good place to be. This document
says what we expect, what we won't tolerate, and what happens when someone
crosses the line. It is written in our own words on purpose — it is short
because the rules are short.

## The standard

Treat the people in this project — issues, pull requests, Discussions,
chat — the way you would want a senior engineer to treat you on a bad day:
with patience, directness, and respect. You can disagree hard about code and
still be kind to the person.

**We expect you to:**

- Be respectful of differing viewpoints, experience levels, and first
  languages. This project has Chinese- and English-speaking participants;
  rough English (in either direction) is not a character flaw.
- Critique ideas, not people. "This approach has a race condition" is review.
  "You clearly don't understand concurrency" is not.
- Assume good faith, and give it back. Most friction is a misunderstanding,
  not malice.
- Accept that maintainers may say "no" or "not now," and that a closed PR is
  not a personal rejection.

**We will not tolerate:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind — including based on
  age, body, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or
  expression, experience level, nationality, appearance, race, religion, or
  sexual identity.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Personal or political attacks, sustained disruption, or deliberate
  intimidation.
- Publishing someone's private information without their explicit permission.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

This applies in all project spaces, and also when you are representing the
project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, report it
**privately to the maintainers**. Do not feel obligated to confront the person
publicly first.

> **Interim reporting channel (pre-1.0):** the project does not yet have a
> dedicated conduct mailbox. Until it does, use GitHub's **private reporting**
> on the repository (Security → report) to reach the maintainers confidentially;
> mark the report as a conduct issue. Establishing a dedicated, clearly-labeled
> conduct contact is a tracked pre-launch task — when it exists, this section
> will name it.

Reports are handled confidentially. We will not name a reporter to the person
reported without the reporter's consent.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for upholding this code and will respond to reports
in good faith. Depending on severity, a response may be:

1. **A private word** — a quiet correction explaining what was wrong and why.
2. **A warning** — a formal notice, with consequences stated if it continues.
3. **A temporary ban** — a time-boxed exclusion from project spaces. No
   interaction with the people involved during this period.
4. **A permanent ban** — for severe violations, or a sustained pattern after
   warnings.

Maintainers who do not uphold this code in good faith may themselves face
consequences, decided by the other maintainers and the steward (see
[`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md)).

We will favor the simplest response that resolves the problem. The point is a
healthy project, not a tribunal.

## Scope and spirit

This code is intentionally lightweight. If a situation arises that it does not
literally cover, apply the spirit: would a thoughtful person consider this
respectful and in good faith? If not, it's out of bounds, listed or not.
